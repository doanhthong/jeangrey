Organizers & Co-organizers

TBIA Capital

TBIA Capital is committed to investment and professional counseling to help Taiwan's new industry development, and to improvement of public welfare. TBIA Capital's goal is to make entrepreneurial ecosystem better, to accelerate the development of high-quality start-ups, and to active industry resources.

Chaoyang Business Incubation Center

CBIC devotes to build a healthy and friendly circumstance for startups by integrating resources of governments, supply chains, capital market, and academies. Not only do we focus on existing startups, but we also help the high-potential students to develop a new scalable business model.

Taiwan Startup hub

Central area Startup hub integrate resources from central and local government (including Miaoli, Taichung, Changhua, Nantou, Yunlin) also combine with nongovernment organizations, to create an all-in-one startup service base. Central area Startup Hub offers individual consultation; furthermore, we hold various activities and courses to share information about startup business. The purpose of Central area Startup hub is to gather entrepreneurs with government and local community for more convenient and abundant connection.

StarFab Accelerator

StarFab was founded in 2016 with the realization that a startup requires more than just an innovative product and funding to successfully establish its position in today’s competitive and fast-paced market. We see ourselves as partners of innovation, helping entrepreneurs gain a footing with large enterprises and enterprises to collaborate with startups to provide added-value for their customers. 75 companies have been carefully selected for our accelerator programs to date, with 40% of alumni receiving external funding, and program graduates aggressively expanding their businesses globally.

Feng Chia University Business Incubation and Technology Licensing Center

Development Objectives:
1. As a part of “R&D, incubation, university-industry collaboration Zone of FCU”, BIC provides enterprises in the CTSP and satellite companies around central Taiwan by integrating business incubation and technology licensing. BIC has been assisting tenants commercializing technologies and technology transfers for 7 years.
2. Working under a unique “Business Incubation + Technology Licensing” model, BIC has provided new and fast services by playing professional and multi-functional roles.
3. Taking advantage of R&D resources, facility, and administrative network from the University, BIC provides local companies technology services.

Taiwan Business Incubators Alliance

TBIA原是於2001年，由台中區創新育成聯盟為提昇創業育成成效，運用策略聯盟共同推動資訊共享、資源交流、經驗分享及跨單位分工等目的，特成立之育成聯盟。歷經十餘年的成長，目前已成為跨北中南東區各縣市之育成聯盟。 為非營利、無會費之育成服務聯盟，成員包括40多家大學和科研機構的創業育成中心。並於中部科學園區設立辦公室。以替我各育成夥伴爭取福利及建立各產官學界合作溝通橋樑為主要目標
